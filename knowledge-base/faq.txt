# Metro Parking Authority - Frequently Asked Questions

## Payment Questions

### Q: How long do I have to pay my violation?
A: You have 30 days from the issue date to pay the original fine amount. After 30 days, a $25 late fee is added. After 60 days, the violation may be sent to collections and additional fees may apply.

### Q: Can I pay part of what I owe?
A: Yes, you can make partial payments. However, your account will remain outstanding until the full balance is paid. Late fees will continue to accrue on unpaid amounts.

### Q: What if my payment doesn't go through?
A: If your payment fails, you'll receive an error message. Please verify your card information and try again, or try a different payment method. Failed payments do not stop late fees from accruing.

### Q: Will I receive a receipt?
A: Yes, a confirmation number will be provided at the end of your call. You can also request an email receipt by providing your email address, or view receipts online at www.metroparking.gov.

## Violation Questions

### Q: I don't recognize this violation. What should I do?
A: First, verify the license plate number and date. If you believe the violation was issued in error, you may submit a dispute. Common reasons include: vehicle was sold, plates were stolen, or the meter malfunctioned.

### Q: What happens if I don't pay?
A: Unpaid violations may result in:
- Late fees ($25 after 30 days)
- Vehicle registration hold
- Boot or tow of vehicle
- Referral to collections agency
- Negative credit reporting

### Q: Can I see a photo of my violation?
A: Photos are not available through the phone system. Please visit www.metroparking.gov or request photos by mail.

## Dispute Questions

### Q: How long does a dispute take?
A: Disputes are typically reviewed within 10-15 business days. You will receive a written response by mail.

### Q: What happens while my dispute is pending?
A: Late fees are paused while a dispute is under review. However, if your dispute is denied, you will be responsible for the full amount including any fees that would have accrued.

### Q: Can I dispute a violation if I already paid it?
A: No, payment is considered an admission of liability. Please submit disputes before making payment.

## General Questions

### Q: How do I update my address?
A: Address updates must be made through the DMV. Metro Parking Authority uses DMV records to send violation notices.

### Q: Can someone else pay my violation?
A: Yes, anyone can make a payment as long as they have the citation number or license plate information.

### Q: Do you offer senior or veteran discounts?
A: Metro Parking Authority does not offer discounts on parking violations. However, certain disability parking exemptions may apply. Please speak with an agent for details.
